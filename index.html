The Problem We're Solving

Technology that could help
them wasn't built for them.

Older adults are surrounded by apps, devices, and services that promise independence — but the interfaces assume fast fingers, sharp eyes, and a comfort with technology that was never designed with them in mind. They don't fail technology. Technology fails them.

The phone is the one interface they've trusted their whole lives. They already know how to use it. They're already comfortable talking into it. But when they call for help — with a phone that's acting up, a text that looks suspicious, or something that's just on their mind — there's no one there who really knows them.

Arthur answers. Not a script. Not a menu. A real conversation — one that remembers who they are, understands what they need, and helps them handle it themselves.

Clear About What This Is

What Arthur is —
and what he isn't.

We'd rather tell you plainly than let you expect something Arthur can't do. Here's the honest line.

What Arthur is

  • ✓ A patient voice on the phone who helps with your devices and the small daily snags
  • ✓ A second opinion when something looks like a scam
  • ✓ Someone who remembers you and picks up where you left off
  • ✓ A sounding board for the things on your mind

What Arthur is not

  • ✕ A doctor, nurse, or medical service — Arthur doesn't diagnose or give medical advice
  • ✕ An emergency line — in an emergency, always call 911
  • ✕ A way to move money, make trades, or handle your accounts for you
  • ✕ A monitor reporting on you to your family behind your back
If it's an emergency, hang up and call 911. Arthur is a companion and a helper — not a medical, emergency, or financial service.
