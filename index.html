Purpose & context Dan is a product-focused founder building Arthur — an AI companion for seniors aging in place, named after his grandfather. The product's core insight is that tech friction, not seniors themselves, is the barrier to adoption: "they aren't failing at tech... tech is failing them." Arthur operates through phone calls and SMS (inbound and outbound), with smart speakers as a deferred secondary channel. The outbound calling capability — Arthur proactively reaching out to seniors rather than waiting to be summoned — is the central differentiator. The product has gone through significant evolution: originally conceived as "Nightingale" with smart speaker delivery as the primary interface, Dan rebranded to Arthur and pivoted to a phone-first architecture after concluding that platform dependency on Amazon/Google represented unacceptable strategic risk and unnecessary build complexity. The "nothing new to learn" brand promise is actually stronger with phone, given near-universal telephone familiarity among seniors 75+. Tech stack direction: Twilio (telephony), Deepgram Nova-2 (STT), ElevenLabs (TTS), Anthropic Claude 3.5 Sonnet (conversation), FastAPI/Python backend, PostgreSQL, APScheduler. Dan has expressed a preference for Anthropic as the LLM provider on safety and ethics grounds. Key product components: Phone-first AI companion for seniors Caregiver dashboard (web + mobile) for family members to train Arthur, monitor wellbeing, receive alerts Device Library feature: caregivers register the senior's devices so Arthur can give specific troubleshooting help Pricing: ~$39/month; target COGS ~$11/user; target gross margin ~72% Dan has completed market research, competitive analysis, user interviews, and full product design. He has a GitHub repo, Cloudflare Pages deployment, and a growing waitlist. He is actively seeking a technical co-founder (offering equity, not a contract) with voice/NLP, Python/FastAPI, Twilio, and LLM/GenAI experience. The Problem We're Solving

Technology that could help
them wasn't built for them.

Seniors are surrounded by apps, devices, and services that promise independence — but the interfaces assume fast fingers, sharp screens, and a familiarity with technology that was never designed with them in mind. They don't fail technology. Technology fails them.

The smart speaker sitting on their kitchen counter is the one exception. They already use it. They're already comfortable talking to it. But it only goes so far — music, timers, weather, and lights. When real life gets complex, it hits a wall.

Arthur doesn't ask seniors to adopt anything new. It upgrades the intelligence of what they already trust — so the speaker that plays their morning playlist can also coordinate their healthcare, protect them from scams, and have a real conversation.

For Families & Caregivers

Stay close,
even from a distance.

Arthur isn't just for seniors. The caregiver dashboard gives family members real-time visibility, smart alerts, and the tools to train Arthur — so it becomes truly personal to your loved one.

Is Mom okay? In under 5 seconds.

A daily status banner tells you everything at a glance — conversations, medications, last contact, and any open concerns. Green means good. No green means you need to know.

Smart alerts, not noise.

Arthur monitors patterns over time — speech changes, unusual silences, repeated confusion, sudden topic shifts. When something feels off, you're the first to know, not the last.

Train Arthur to know her.

Add medications, preferred topics, family names, even her devices — so Arthur gives specific, personal help instead of generic answers. The more you teach it, the better it gets.

Built-in device library.

Snap a photo of her TV, phone, or tablet and Arthur identifies the exact model. No more guesswork — Arthur knows her Samsung remote has a known lag issue before she even calls.
